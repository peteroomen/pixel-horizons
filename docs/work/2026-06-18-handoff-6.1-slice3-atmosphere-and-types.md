## TL;DR

Slice 2 made the **rock** match the planet (tiles + deposit hosts + debris FX + the cave-mouth
backdrop rock). Two visible gaps remain, both seeded and both small:

1. **The sky doesn't match.** `surfaceBackdrop`'s `skyLayer` (peach sunset gradient + sun +
   horizon mesas) is still hardcoded warm. On a green/ice planet you drop into a green cave
   under an orange sky — the one remaining "off" note. This was explicitly deferred from slice 2
   as "atmosphere."
2. **There is only one planet *type*.** `data/planets.ts` has just `terran`; `planetRamps` only
   emits terran ocean/land ramps. Volcanic/Ice (roadmap 5.3) are the obvious next variety, and
   they're what makes the recolor pipeline (already built) actually pay off across a run.

Pick **one** of these as the slice (don't do both — keep it shippable). My recommendation order
and reasoning below.

## Option A — Atmosphere/sky recolor (smaller, finishes slice 2's look)

The seam is identical to slice 2. `skyLayer(g, W, H)` in `src/renderer/surface-sprites.ts` is
the only fixed-warm layer left; `surfaceBackdrop(W, H, P)` already receives the ramp `P` and
passes it to `midLayer`/`nearLayer`. Just thread `P` into `skyLayer` too.

What's in `skyLayer` today (all hardcoded):
- Sky gradient: `mix('#fdcbb0', '#cd683d', y/H)` — warm peach→rust.
- Sun: a `blob` in `mix('#fbff86', '#fbb954', 0.3)` — warm yellow.
- Horizon mesas: `mix('#ab947a', '#cd683d', 0.4)` bodies + `#c7dcd0` lit tops.

Decisions to make (and why this needs a *little* thought, not just a find-replace):
- **A sky ramp ≠ the land ramp.** The land ramp is for rock; a sky wants a *light, desaturated*
  gradient (horizon glow → zenith). Cleanest is a new `skyRampFor(descriptor)` (or extend
  `planetRamps` to emit a `sky` ramp) next to `surfaceRampFor` in `palette.ts`, palette-locked
  to R64. Don't just reuse `land` — it'll read as muddy.
- **Keep the sun warm or tint it?** A warm sun on every world reads as "a sun"; tinting it per
  star colour is a nice touch but scope-creeps toward a star/space-bg generator (later slice).
  Recommend: keep the sun a fixed warm disc for now, recolor only the sky gradient + mesas.
- **Mesas are distant terrain** — they can reasonably take the land ramp's *lighter* steps
  (atmospheric perspective: distant = lighter/desaturated), so they read as far hills of the
  same rock. That ties sky and ground together.
- **Readability:** the sky is the brightest thing on screen and the platformer reads silhouettes
  against it. Keep the sky clearly lighter than the terrain ramp so platforms stay legible
  (slice 2's `nearLayer` darks already help). Test on a *dark* land ramp (tundra) where the
  contrast is tightest.

Verify exactly like slice 2: `?mode=orbit&seed=…` → DROP → sky hue family matches the planet;
same `?seed=` reproduces; 375px legible; `?mode=surface` fallback fine.

## Option B — Volcanic + Ice planet types (roadmap 5.3, bigger, higher payoff)

The recolor pipeline is built and waiting. Adding a type is now mostly data + a switch:
- `data/planets.ts`: add `'volcanic' | 'ice'` to `PlanetType` + entries in `PLANET_TYPES` and
  `ALL_PLANET_TYPES` (this is what `planetForNode`'s RNG picks from — adding types changes the
  planet distribution per seed, which is fine; still deterministic, still derived).
- `renderer/palette.ts`: `planetRamps` currently picks from `OCEAN_RAMPS`/`LAND_RAMPS` by seed
  with no type input. To make *types* visually distinct, give `planetRamps` (and/or
  `surfaceRampFor`) a per-type ramp family: volcanic = the `VOLCANIC` ramp already in
  `surface-sprites.ts` (`#fbff86…#6e2727`), ice = `ICE` (`#c7dcd0…#323353`). Both are already
  6-step R64 ramps in the exact format. Branch in `surfaceRampFor`'s `switch` (the slot is there)
  and feed the shader the matching ocean/land.
- **5.3 also wants hazards** (volcanic vents already exist as the sandstorm-vent mechanic; ice
  = slippery?). Scope that separately or defer — the *visual* type is the cheap win; hazards are
  the gameplay half. Recommend: ship the visual types first, hazards as a follow-on.

⚠️ Coordinate the shader: `renderer/planet.ts` selects the generator family by `descriptor.type`
(check `buildPlanetMesh` — today it ignores type and only uses `planetRamps(seed)`). A volcanic
planet should *look* volcanic from orbit too (lava cracks / no ocean), not just be a recoloured
terran. That's the real work in Option B and why it's bigger.

## Gotchas to carry forward

- **Verify against the generated canvas, not just the screen.** Slice 2 burned time thinking the
  recolor was broken — the test level `ROCKY_TEST_LEVEL` is ~90% bedrock (fixed) and the backdrop
  dominated, so on-screen change looked nil. Pixel-reading `someTile(ramp).getContext('2d')
  .getImageData(...)` proved the factory was correct. Use that trick to separate
  "factory wrong" from "not visible here."
- **WebGL canvas readback returns black** (no `preserveDrawingBuffer`) — use `preview_screenshot`
  for on-screen colour, and read the *source* canvas for factory correctness.
- **Determinism / no version bump:** descriptors stay derived (`planetForNode`). Don't add planet
  state to `RunState`. Adding types to `ALL_PLANET_TYPES` changes what a given seed rolls — that's
  expected and fine; just don't persist it.
- **Two palettes, one law:** keep new ramps muted R64; pure accents stay reserved for signals.

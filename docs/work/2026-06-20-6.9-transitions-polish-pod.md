## Goal

Ship both a feel/design polish pass on the existing Lane Drop / Lane Launch transitions and the new Drop Pod Deploy (orbit→surface) transition, built on the same driver, in one PR.

## Approach

**Part 1 — Spiral polish (all in `src/renderer/transition.ts`):**
- Densify the spiral by raising SPIRAL_POINTS to 140 per arm and adding a second interleaved arm (theta offset π) — effectively 280 total points vs 150, making the tunnel read as continuous rather than scattered.
- Speed curves stay unchanged; the feel pass (smoothness/timing) needs human eyes at 60fps — flagged in PR.

**Part 2 — Slice B: Drop Pod Deploy, kind=`'pod-deploy'`:**
The approved minimal-handoff design: Scene A (orbit view: ship + planet + pod detaches and shrinks toward planet), A→B wipe (full-frame amber heat-glow masks the cut), Scene B (pod streaks in from above with amber trail + orange retro-burn), impact (1-frame red flash + 3px deterministic camera shake), settle (onComplete → enterSurface).

- New constants: `AMBER = 0xffb454`, `IMPACT_RED = 0xff4757`, `POD_DEPLOY_MS = 3600`.
- New layers: `amberLayer` (full-frame amber rect, above `world`, below flashes), `impactFlash` (full-frame red rect, top).
- Pod asset: `podLaunchSprite()` from `surface-sprites.ts` — passed from main.ts as `assets.pod`, renderer stays asset-agnostic.
- Reduced motion: quick amber fade-in/fade-out (no animation), then onComplete.
- Wiring: `dropToSurface()` in main.ts wraps `runTransition('pod-deploy', ...)` → `enterSurface`. `resolveDevTransition()` extended to accept `'pod-deploy'`. Dev knob loop handles pod-deploy assets.

## What actually happened

Built as planned, no major surprises.

**Spiral polish:** `SPIRAL_POINTS` reduced to 140 per arm; a second arm added with theta offset π, giving 280 total draw calls vs 150 before. The two interleaved arms produce a continuous tunnel feel — each arm sweeps the same angular range but at opposite phase, so there are no visible gaps. Color alternation kept per-point (i%2) on each arm independently, producing a complementary striped pattern between arms.

**Pod Deploy timeline (3.6s):** Implemented the full 5-phase timeline against the approved spec:
- Scene A (0–1.3s): ship fades in at orbit pose, planet at centre; pod fades in docked near ship, detaches at 0.7s with a brief retro-puff (orange additive wash circle), then easeInCubic falls toward planet centre while shrinking (scale 0.6→0.12).
- A→B wipe (1.3–2.0s): amber heat-glow radiates from planet centre via 3-ring additive circles; `amberLayer` (separate full-frame amber rect above `world`) easeInCubic fades to alpha=1, masking the scene cut.
- Scene B (2.0–2.8s): `amberLayer` easeOutCubic fades out; pod sprite rotated π (nose-down) enters from y=-30 and easeOutCubic decelerates to CY+20; amber heat trail drawn above pod; orange retro-burn glow fires from bT>0.45.
- Impact (2.8–3.0s): `impactFlash` (separate full-frame red rect, top of stack) easeOutCubic fades; 3px deterministic sine/cosine camera shake on `world.position` decays with impT.
- Settle (3.0–3.6s): pod easeOutCubic fades; onComplete fires at t=1 → enterSurface.
- Reduced motion: quick amber fade-in/fade-out (0→1→0 over REDUCED_MS), no long animation.

**Layer stack order:** bg → world (wash+spiral+planetSprite+podSprite+ship) → amberLayer → flash (cyan, lane transitions) → impactFlash (red, pod-deploy). All layers always created; only the relevant ones are used per transition kind.

**main.ts wiring:** `podLaunchSprite` imported from surface-sprites; `resolveDevTransition()` extended for `'pod-deploy'`; devTransition loop supplies `{ ship, planet, pod }` for pod-deploy; `dropToSurface()` wraps `runTransition('pod-deploy', { ship, planet: currentPlanet ?? undefined, pod }, () => enterSurface())` replacing the prior direct `enterSurface()` call.

Lint clean, type-check clean, 529 tests green (no new tests added — the driver is renderer code, not sim logic).
